The moment you need a web app planning guide is right now. If you're over 50, know nothing about coding, but have the passion to "create your own app," you've come to the right place. You can forget about fancy technical terms and complex tools for now.

In fact, the rich life experience and work know-how of people in their 50s is a powerful weapon that young planners don't have. We'll guide you through realistic planning methods to solve 'real problems' like the hassle of organizing documents and forgetting health records.

1. Finding 'Everyday Problems' Worth Solving (Discovery)

Successful web apps don't come from cutting-edge technology. They start from specific everyday inconveniences. Carry a small notebook for a week and record every moment you think "Can't this be easier?"

  • Blood pressure/blood sugar recording journal to show your doctor
  • Family events and memorial day schedule management that's easy to forget
  • Side business customer/sales ledger started after retirement
  • Personal book review and transcription notes

2. Transforming Rough Ideas into 'Clear Concepts'

Once you've picked one of the problems you collected, it's time to refine it into language that developers (or no-code tools) can understand. The difference between vague thinking and concrete planning lies in 'clarity.'

Category Vague Idea (X) Clear Concept (O)
One-sentence Definition Just a good health management app An app that records blood pressure and shows monthly changes to doctors
Target User Everyone Men in their 50-60s who need to manage high blood pressure
Core Action Makes you healthy automatically Open the app and input today's readings within 30 seconds

3. Creating a 'Feature Roadmap' on a Single Sheet of Paper

It's like drawing blueprints before laying bricks when building a house. The foundation of an app is defining 'Must-have features'. Save the fancy decorations (Nice-to-have) for later.
