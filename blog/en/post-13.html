1. Choose a Small, Realistic Problem ü§î

When you decide to build a side project web app in your 50s, the most important choice isn't which tool to use‚Äîit's which problem to solve. Beginners often dream of marketplaces or large-scale booking systems, but these quickly become overwhelming for those of us with jobs and families.

Instead, look closely at your daily routines. Small, repeated inconveniences like "tracking shared expenses and viewing settlement history" or "recording daily blood pressure and mood" become excellent project seeds.

üí° Planning Tip: Paper Sketching
Before opening any coding tool, grab a pen and draw the screen. A friendly title, input box, save button, list. When you design the conversation between user and app on paper first, development time is cut in half.

Once your sketch is ready, it's time to create a basic working first version. At this stage, design and advanced features don't matter. Just achieve this one thing: "Can I input, save, and view it again?"
